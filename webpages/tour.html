Jahaz Mahal, Mandu

Located on the northern side of Munj Talao (Tank) in the royal Mandu complex, Jahaz Mahal or water palace has 2 halls with special valued ceilings. The walls are made up of marble and have several medals in yellow and blue along with inscriptions. Though in ruins, it still looks captivating. Although not much is known about the time of its construction but it is believed that it was built after the Khalji era, may be to give an intimate place to the royal couples. However, it is also believed that it is one of the luxury retreats of the Sultan of Malwa. This palace comprises of tiny baolis (water wells) in the courtyard. The whole complex is connected to it through a pathway.

Gwalior fort, Gwalior

One of the most popular attractions in Madhya Pradesh, the massive Gwalior fort is over 820 m in width, 2.4 km in length, and 104 m in height, offering stunning views. A stunning medieval architecture, the fort has several splendid structures in its premises such as Jain temples, Saas Bahu temple, Teli ka Mandir, Suraj Kund, Hathi Pol, Gujari Mahal etc. The even sound and light show is a sheer delight. This one definitely counts as one of the finest forts and palaces in Madhya Pradesh..Entry Fees: INR 5/- per person (Indian) and INR 100 per person (Foreigner) Camera: Rs. 25/- Timings– 06:00 AM–05:30 PM.

Chanderi Fort, Chanderi

Built during the 11th century, the Chanderi fort is surrounded by hills, lakes, and forests. The fort is placed towards the South-west of the Betwa River atop a hill, at a height of 71 m. It is also surrounded the monuments that were owned by the Malwa Sultans and the Bundela Rajputs. Placed on the borders of Bundelkhand and Malwa, Chanderi even finds a mention in the great Hindu epic, Mahbharata. It was then ruled by Shishupal. A massive fort, it has a huge gate that was famously known as the ‘Khooni Darwaza’. During their rule, the Muslim kings commissioned the fortification walls. The fort also has an inquisitive gateway towards its southwest, called Katti Ghatti, made via hill sides. Read More Entry– N/A Timings– Dawn to Dusk.

Lal Bagh Palace, Indore

One of the finest structures built by the rulers of the Holkar dynasty, Lal Bagh Palace was erected between 1886 and 1921 by Maharaja Shivaji Rao Holkar. The construction of the palace took place in three phases. Spread in a garden of 28 hectares, the palace’s gates seems to be a replica of the Buckingham Palace of the United Kingdom. It used to be the venue of lavish royal receptions hosted by the Holkars. This beautiful structure has influences of European styles including a Palladian queen’s bedroom, baroque and rococo dining rooms, an elegant Renaissance sitting room with ragged sofas, and an English library that has chairs with leather armrests. While on the ground floor, there are prehistoric artifacts, the first floor has a coin collection dating back to Muslim era. The mighty palace has other amazing displays too.
